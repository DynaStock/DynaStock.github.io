Key Findings

🧠 Finding 1: Reasoning Patterns are Model-Dependent

Models operating in thinking (CoT) mode show varied performance. Kimi-k2.5 improves from 27.63% to 35.95% (+8.32 pp) with thinking enabled, while DeepSeek V3.2 exhibits the opposite pattern, suggesting that the thinking premium is model-dependent rather than universally beneficial.

🌍 Finding 2: Strategies and Risk Attitudes are Market-Dependent

Strategy scaffolding and risk attitudes exhibit market-dependent effectiveness. Models show different optimal configurations across A-share, HK, and US markets, reflecting the need for market-specific adaptation.

📈 Finding 3: Asymmetric Trend Adaptation

Models exhibit asymmetry in trend adaptation: they underperform benchmarks in bull markets (A-share +50.95%) but demonstrate superior risk management in bear markets (US -9.39%), with conservative strategies showing smaller losses.
