DynaStock: A Dynamic Benchmark for Strategic Adaptability of
Large Language Models in Multimodal Real-World Finance

Anonymous ACL Submission

TL;DR

DynaStock bridges the gap between static benchmarks and real-world reliability by evaluating financial LLMs through dynamic, closed-loop trading simulations, exposing critical failures in strategic adaptability under high volatility.

Abstract

Current evaluation paradigms for financial Large Language Models (LLMs) rely heavily on static historical datasets and opaque profitability metrics, failing to capture strategic adaptability in real-world trading. This limitation conceals a critical disconnect between reported performance and practical reliability. To address this, we introduce DynaStock, a benchmark that transforms evaluation into a longitudinal auditing process. By employing a perpetual forward-testing methodology under a graded strategy scaffolding mechanism, DynaStock distinguishes between accidental profitability and genuine strategic compliance. The framework integrates multimodal data construction and closed-loop trading simulations across three major global markets to evaluate the mapping between market context and executable decisions. Extensive experiments with state-of-the-art models (ranging from 14B to 235B parameters) expose a significant capability gap: even LLMs achieving impressive results on traditional benchmarks exhibit profound deficiencies in maintaining strategic consistency under high-volatility scenarios. These findings underscore the limitations of current models as autonomous financial agents.

Figure 1: Overview of the DynaStock Framework. The pipeline integrates three core modules: (1) Multimodal Data Construction (prices, news, reports); (2) Graded Strategy Scaffolding (injecting expert strategies like Reversal/Momentum); and (3) a Closed-Loop Execution Engine that enforces hard constraints to validate strategic adaptability.

Figure 2: Global Market and Sector Coverage. DynaStock utilizes multimodal data from three major global markets: A-shares, Hong Kong, and U.S. equities. The dataset covers diverse industries (Financials, Technology, etc.), challenging agents to adapt to different market microstructures.

Table 1: Comparison with Existing Financial Benchmarks. Unlike previous static benchmarks (e.g., FinBen, StockBench), DynaStock employs a longitudinal 2025 dataset across multiple markets (A-share, HK, US) with Multimodal inputs (News, Reports, Time-series) to ensure zero-lookahead bias.

Table 2: Short-term Tactical Adaptability (RQ1). Performance across 15-day horizons. Note the "Scaffolding Premium" (highlighted rows): models in Strict Mode achieve significantly higher Sharpe Ratios and lower Max Drawdowns compared to Free Mode, showing the benefit of strategic constraints.

Figure 3: Impact of Fundamental Data (RQ2). Scaling laws over a 90-day horizon. Agents utilizing Annual Reports (orange line) consistently outperform Technical-Only agents (blue line), with the gap widening as model size increases to 235B, demonstrating the value of fundamental awareness.

Table 3: Resilience to Market Shocks (RQ3). During extreme volatility, larger models (Qwen3-235B) show superior Recovery Factors (1.48) compared to smaller models, indicating stronger capability to recover from drawdowns without "panic trading".
