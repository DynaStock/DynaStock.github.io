DynaStock: A Dynamic Benchmark for Strategic Adaptability of Large Language Models in Realistic Multi-Market Trading

Anonymous Submission
Code & Dataset Project Page

TL;DR

DynaStock is a rolling evaluation framework that audits the adaptive reasoning and strategic compliance of LLMs in continuous, multi-market trading. By introducing Graded Strategy and Attitude Scaffolding, it distinguishes genuine financial reasoning from unconstrained gambling on memorized patterns.

Abstract

Current evaluation paradigms for Large Language Models (LLMs) in the financial sector rely heavily on static question-answering datasets. Furthermore, simulated trading evaluations frequently neglect real-world market frictions, such as minimum trading lot sizes and capital constraints. These limitations render existing evaluations inadequate for reflecting a model's adaptive capabilities within authentic, continuous trading environments, leading to a severe disconnect between public benchmark performance and real-world deployment reliability.

To address this critical gap, we propose DynaStock: the first long-term, closed-loop trading evaluation benchmark grounded in multi-source heterogeneous data and a graded strategy architecture. We construct fine-grained datasets encompassing multiple core global markets, employing recent time windows to mitigate data memorization biases. Additionally, we incorporate stocks exhibiting both upward and downward trends to comprehensively assess the models' decision-making logic across diverse market dynamics.

DynaStock introduces Graded Strategy and Attitude Scaffolding, requiring models to explicitly output their selected strategy alongside the rationale for their trading decisions. This precisely quantifies their compliance and execution capabilities, supported by a comprehensive suite of native financial metrics.

Based on an extensive evaluation of several state-of-the-art LLMs using this benchmark, we reveal the following key insights: (1) reasoning patterns are model-dependent; (2) strategies and risk attitudes are market-dependent; and (3) models exhibit an asymmetry in trend adaptation. In conclusion, the application of LLMs in financial trading presents concurrent challenges and opportunities. The primary challenge lies in the generally suboptimal performance of current models. Conversely, the opportunity stems from their ability to effectively manage risk during drawdowns, alongside the potential to reshape their trading capabilities through diverse prompting strategies.

Key Contributions

🔄 Continuously Evolving Benchmark

A longitudinal auditing framework that eliminates look-ahead bias through perpetual forward-testing. Integrates multi-market, multi-lingual, and multi-source data to authentically reflect global market complexity.

🔍 White-Box Auditing Mechanism

Graded Strategy and Attitude Scaffolding provides the first systematic quantification of an autonomous agent's compliance capability, effectively decoupling genuine financial reasoning from unconstrained accidental profitability.

📊 Comprehensive Empirical Insights

Extensive experiments reveal significant market-dependent variations in reasoning efficacy and strategic adaptability. Models show exceptional risk mitigation in downtrends, highlighting their potential as intelligent trading assistants.

Key Findings

🧠 Finding 1: Reasoning Patterns are Model-Dependent

Models operating in thinking (CoT) mode show varied performance. Kimi-k2.5 improves from 27.63% to 35.95% (+8.32 pp) with thinking enabled, while DeepSeek V3.2 exhibits the opposite pattern, suggesting that the thinking premium is model-dependent rather than universally beneficial.

🌍 Finding 2: Strategies and Risk Attitudes are Market-Dependent

Strategy scaffolding and risk attitudes exhibit market-dependent effectiveness. Models show different optimal configurations across A-share, HK, and US markets, reflecting the need for market-specific adaptation.

📈 Finding 3: Asymmetric Trend Adaptation

Models exhibit asymmetry in trend adaptation: they underperform benchmarks in bull markets (A-share +50.95%) but demonstrate superior risk management in bear markets (US -9.39%), with conservative strategies showing smaller losses.
